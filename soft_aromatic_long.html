Jasmine Rice

Jasmine rice is grown primarily in Thailand, Laos, and southern Vietnam. It is moist and soft in texture when cooked, with a slightly sweet flavor.

Eating Quality: Soft

Aroma: Aromatic

Grain Size: Long

Meal Type: Side Dish

Water-Cup Ratio: 1 1/2 cups of water : 1 cup of rice

Cooking Time: 25-35 minutes

Cooking Method:

You can cook this rice variety as you would regular white rice: In a medium, heavy-bottomed pot, saucepan, or rice cooker.

Robinson's Supersavers Intan Rice

Intan rice is a variety known for its good malting characteristics. In addition, its malt has a pleasant aroma and an appealing color. Due to these qualities, the Intan rice variety was used for bulk malting and the preparation of malt flour.

Eating Quality: Soft

Aroma: Aromatic

Grain Size: Long

Meal Type: Side Dish

Water-Cup Ratio: 1 cup of water : 1 cup of rice

Cooking Time: 10-25 minutes

Harvester's Sinandomeng Rice

Harvester's Sinandomeng Rice is your every-day and every occasion rice. Sinandomeng is a traditional Filipino favorite, the long and slender grains when cooked produce a delicious aroma and soft texture. Because of its versatility, you can also prepare it as fried rice or congee.

Eating Quality: Soft

Aroma: Aromatic

Grain Size: Long

Meal Type: Side Dish

Water-Cup Ratio: 1 1/2 cups of water : 1 cup of rice

Cooking Time: 10-25 minutes

Cooking Method:

You can cook this rice variety as you would regular white rice: In a medium, heavy-bottomed pot, saucepan, or rice cooker.
